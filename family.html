Mercado-Rizal Family

The Mercado-Rizal family was one of the most influential and respected families in Calamba, Laguna during the Spanish colonial period. The surname 'Mercado' was adopted by Jose Rizal's great-grandfather, Domingo Lam-co, a Chinese immigrant, to symbolize their trade (market) profession. Later, Jose's father, Francisco, adopted the additional surname 'Rizal' to comply with a Spanish decree on surnames and to avoid confusion with other Mercados.

Francisco Mercado Rizal (1818-1898)

Francisco Mercado Rizal was Jose Rizal's father, a prosperous farmer who encouraged his children's education and instilled in them a love for learning and patriotism.

Teodora Alonso Realonda (1827-1911)

Teodora Alonso Realonda was Jose Rizal's mother, a highly educated and strong-willed woman who greatly influenced Jose's intellectual development and nationalist ideas.

Saturnina Rizal (1850-1913)

Saturnina Rizal was the eldest sister of Jose Rizal. Known for her strong personality, she played a significant role in the family and was often referred to as 'Neneng'.

Paciano Rizal (1851-1930)

Paciano Rizal was the elder brother of Jose Rizal. A close confidant and supporter, he was involved in the reform movement and greatly influenced Jose's nationalist ideas.

Narcisa Rizal (1852-1939)

Narcisa Rizal was one of Jose Rizal's older sisters. Known as 'Sisa', she was known for her caring nature and helped manage the family's finances and estate.

Olympia Rizal (1855-1887)

Olympia Rizal was another sister of Jose Rizal. She was known for her wit and intelligence and was very close to her brother Jose.

Lucia Rizal (1857-1919)

Lucia Rizal was another sibling of Jose Rizal. She supported Jose's reformist ideals and was known for her quiet strength.

Maria Rizal (1859-1945)

Maria Rizal was another sibling of Jose Rizal. She was supportive of her brother's endeavors and played a key role in maintaining the family's unity.

Jose Rizal (1861-1896)

Jose Rizal was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary. His works and martyrdom greatly influenced the Philippine revolution against Spanish colonization.

Concepcion Rizal (1862-1865)

Concepcion Rizal, also known as 'Concha', was one of Jose Rizal's sisters who died young. Her death had a profound impact on Jose during his childhood.

Josefa Rizal (1865-1945)

Josefa Rizal was another sibling of Jose Rizal. Known as 'Panggoy', she was a strong advocate for women's rights and supported her brother's cause for Philippine independence.

Trinidad Rizal (1868-1951)

Trinidad Rizal was another sibling of Jose Rizal. She played a significant role in preserving Jose's letters and writings, contributing to his enduring legacy.

Soledad Rizal (1870-1929)

Soledad Rizal was the youngest sibling of Jose Rizal. She was known for her musical talents and was a staunch supporter of her brother's nationalist aspirations.
