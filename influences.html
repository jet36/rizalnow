Rizal's Influences

.

.

Don Francisco

Jose learned the value of respect, love for work, and independent thinking from his father, Don Francisco.

Doña Teodora

From his mother, Doña Teodora, Jose got a high sense of self-sacrifice, religiosity, and love for the arts.

Paciano

His freedom and justice were inculcated in him by Paciano.

Uncle Gregorio

Jose learned other valuable lessons in life. Uncle Gregorio influenced him to love for education and to be hardworking.

Uncle Jose

Uncle Jose encouraged him to develop his skills in painting, sculpting, and sketching.

Leoncio Lopez

Another person who influenced Rizal was Fr. Leoncio Lopez, who taught him to become an independent thinker of wide intelligence and fair judgement.

Rizal's feelings towards these influential figures would have been a mix of respect, gratitude, admiration, and inspiration. Their teachings and guidance shaped his values, principles, and worldview, influencing his contributions to Philippine history and his advocacy for social reform. Jose Rizal's interactions with them also underscored a profound sense of responsibility and duty. Their teachings not only shaped his personal values and principles but also profoundly influenced his perception of societal issues and injustices. This influence drove Rizal to become not just a scholar and artist but also a vocal advocate for social reforms in the Philippines during the late 19th century.
